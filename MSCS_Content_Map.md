*** GRADE 8 REPLACEMENT CONTENT (32 pages) ***
  Lesson 1: The Rise and Expansion (1299-1517) — replaces G8 V-3 L3
    - Origins of Ottoman Empire from Central Asian Turkoman tribes
    - Battle of Manzikert (1071) — Seljuk victory
    - Uthman I founds Ottoman state (1299)
    - Conquest of Constantinople (1453) by Sultan Muhammad al-Fatih
    - Expansion into Arab Lands (1516-1517) by Sultan Selim I
    - Title "Khadim al-Haramayn al-Sharifayn"
    - Activities: Digital timeline, historical maps, Google Maps tracing, discussion
    - Quiz + Glossary

  Lesson 2: Managing the Empire & Rivalry with Europe
    - Province system and Beys
    - Janissaries as elite soldiers
    - Ottoman Navy and defending Muslim lands
    - Piri Reis — navigator and mapmaker
    - Land Power vs Sea Power comparison
    - Siege of Vienna (1529)
    - Activities: Venn diagram, map comparison, collaborative research, argumentative writing
    - Quiz + Key Dates + Glossary

  Lesson 4, Part 1: Suleyman I and His Conquests — replaces G8 V-3 L4 part
    - Who was Sultan Suleyman I (1520-1566)
    - Title "Qanuni" (the Lawgiver) — correct meaning in Islamic governance
    - Conquests: Battle of Mohacs (1526), Siege of Vienna (1529), Iraq from Safavids (1534)
    - North Africa: Algiers (Barbarossa), Tripoli (1551)
    - Territories inherited from Selim I
    - Activities: Digital timeline, historical maps
    - Quiz + Key Dates + Glossary

  Lesson 4, Part 2: Ottoman Istanbul & Architecture — replaces G8 V-3 L4 part
    - Constantinople to Istanbul transformation
    - Suleymaniye Mosque Complex (1550-1559)
    - Mimar Sinan — greatest Ottoman architect (c.1488-1588)
    - Internal dome support vs. flying buttresses (key distinction)
    - Comparison: Suleymaniye Mosque vs St. Peter's Basilica
    - Other works by Mimar Sinan
    - Activities: Venn diagram, virtual mosque tour, fill-in-the-blanks, heritage discussion
    - Quiz + Key Dates + Glossary

ASSESSMENT STRUCTURE (per Curriculum Mapping)
================================================================================
- Diagnostic Assessment: Week 1 (start of academic year)
- Prior Knowledge Check: Built into each lesson (KWL Chart activation)
- Formative Assessment: Exit Tickets, Think-Pair-Share, Quick Write, Observation
- Summative Assessment: Unit Quizzes (every 2 lessons), Mid-Term Exams, Final Exams
- DOK Distribution: 30% DOK1, 30% DOK2, 30% DOK3, 10% DOK4
- Only 45 minutes per week (1 lesson) — every lesson must count
- Total: 24 instructional lessons per year (T1: 10, T2: 7, T3: 7)
